Learn more about the Day of the Dead
Discover some traditions of this celebration in Latin America.
2 of 9
Offerings in Mexico
Mexico celebrates this special day by making offerings or altars to remember their deceased ancestors with photographs, food, personal belongings and different decorative elements.
3 of 9
Giant Barrels in Guatemala
In Guatemala, giant barrels are raised that symbolize the souls of the dead who rise to heaven.
4 of 9
Pan de muerto in El Salvador
El Salvador celebrates this day by preparing "Pan de muerto" which commemorates the sweetness of the memories of the deceased ancestors.
5 of 9
Sugar skulls in Costa Rica and Panama
In Costa Rica and Panama, sugar skulls are prepared, especially dedicated to a deceased ancestor. The tradition is to place the name of the deceased ancestor on the forehead of the skulls.
6 of 9
Day of the Dead in Ecuador
In Ecuador it is customary to drink "Colada morada" and "Guaguas de pan", two traditional meals of the time to commemorate the deceased relatives.
7 of 9
Tantawawas in Bolivia and Peru
In many places in Bolivia and Peru the Day of the Dead is celebrated by preparing "Tantawawas", sweet bread figures that symbolize an offering of the living for their deceased.
8 of 9
Day of the Dead in South America
Decorating with flowers is a very common Day of the Dead activity in most Latin American countries. In many South American countries, graves are visited, cleaned and decorated with flowers to remember deceased relatives.
9 of 9
Day of the Dead in the Caribbean
In the Caribbean, the traditional sandwiches "Huesos de santo" and "Buñuelos de viento" are cooked, which according to tradition are the way in which the living wish their ancestors a good trip and stay in the other world.
